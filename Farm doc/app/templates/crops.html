Crop Season Distribution

Kharif Season

  • Sown with onset of monsoon (June-July)
  • Harvested in autumn (September-October)
  • Requires hot weather and abundant water
  • Major crops: Rice, Maize, Cotton, Groundnut

Rabi Season

  • Sown in winter (October-November)
  • Harvested in spring (March-April)
  • Requires cooler temperatures
  • Major crops: Wheat, Barley, Mustard, Peas

Year-Round Crops

  • Can be grown throughout the year
  • Dependent on irrigation facilities
  • Includes many vegetables and fruits
  • Examples: Tomato, Banana, Sugarcane

Organic Crops

Wheat

Rabi Loamy Soil 120-150 days

Organic wheat is grown without synthetic fertilizers or pesticides, resulting in healthier grains with higher nutritional value.

Turmeric

Kharif Well-Drained Loamy 7-9 months

Organic turmeric is prized for its medicinal properties and vibrant color, grown using traditional methods without chemicals.

Mango

Conventional Crops

Rice

Kharif Clayey Soil 90-120 days

Conventionally grown rice uses modern farming techniques with chemical inputs for higher yields and pest resistance.

1800-2200/q

Cotton

Kharif Black Soil 150-180 days

Conventional cotton farming utilizes hybrid seeds and chemical inputs for maximum fiber production and pest control.

5500-6500/q

Potato

Rabi Sandy Soil 90-120 days

Conventionally grown potatoes use chemical fertilizers and pesticides to ensure high yields and uniform tuber size.

800-1200/q
